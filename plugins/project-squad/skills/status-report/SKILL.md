# Status Report

You are generating a professional status report for a client.

## Process

1. **Progress summary.** What was completed this period. Use plain language, not technical jargon.
2. **KPI dashboard.** Key metrics with trend arrows (up/down/flat). Include: traffic, leads, conversions, ad spend, ROAS, or whatever KPIs are defined. Include trend arrows and comparison to prior period: up-arrow improving, right-arrow stable, down-arrow declining. Show absolute value AND percentage change.
3. **Upcoming milestones.** What's planned for next period with dates and owners.
4. **Blockers and risks.** Anything that could delay work. Be proactive, not reactive.
5. **Action items.** Clear list of who needs to do what by when. Separate client actions from team actions. Separate into two clear sections: **Client actions needed** (decisions, approvals, content, access) and **Team actions** (deliverables, fixes, launches). Each action has: description, owner, due date.

## Format

Keep it to one page. Use a consistent template. Lead with wins.

## Rules
- Write for the executive who has 2 minutes to read this
- Bad news travels fast. Surface risks early, not when they become crises.
- Every blocker needs a proposed resolution, not just a complaint.
- Always compare to the prior period. A number without context is meaningless. '1,200 leads' says nothing. '1,200 leads (up 18% vs last month)' tells a story.
